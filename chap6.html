Patterns and Isometries



Why is this even Math?

Math includes the study of patterns and patterns may be numerical, logical or geometric.



Transformation and Isometries

A transformation is defined as a process which shifts points of the plane to perhaps new locations on the plane.

It has four (4) main types: Translation, Reflection, Rotation and Dilation

Translation, Reflection and Rotation do not change the image. While Dilation expands and contracts the pre-image.

There are four (4) types of plane isometries: Translation, Reflection, Rotation and Glide Reflection.

  • Translation: Moves a shape in a given direction by sliding it vertically, diagonally or horizontally.

    • Credit: ck12
  • Reflection: A reflection is a flipped or mirrored version of the image.
    • Credit: ck12
  • Rotation: This has a point and angle showing where and how far the rotation goes.
    • Credit: Math Is Fun
  • Dilation: Changes the size of an object.
    • Credit: MathBits

  • Isometries are defined as transformations which leaves the dimensions of an image unchanged.

  • Comes from the Greek words “isos” which means equal and “metron” which means measure.

  • Isometries are also called isometric transformations or rigid transformation.

  • Glide Reflections are combinations of two transformations, a reflection and a translation.

Fractals




Symmetric Patterns

A plane pattern has symmetry if there is an isometry of the plane that preserves it.

Types of symmetric patterns:
  • Rosette patterns
  • Frieze patterns
  • Wallpaper patterns

Rosette Patterns

Finite groups of isometries which can contain just rotations and reflections

Cyclic: Cn - has n-fold rotational symmetry and no reflectional symmetry.

Dihedral: Dn - has n-fold rotational symmetry and reflectional symmetry.

Credit: Francis Campena

Frieze Patterns

Infinitely long imprinted with a design given by a repeating motif.

Basically just copies of an image wrapping around something.

Frieze Groups

  1. p111 - Only translation
  2. p1a1 - Glide Reflection
  3. p1m1 - Horizontal Reflection
  4. pm11 -Vertical Reflection
  5. p112 - Half Turn
  6. pmm2 - Horizontal and Vertical Reflections
  7. pma2 - Glide Reflection and Vertical Reflection

Wallpaper Patterns

Covers the plane and can be mapped onto itself by translations in more than one direction.

To qualify as a wallpaper pattern, it must have:
  • At least the basic unit
  • One copy by translation
  • A copy of the formed image by translation in a second direction

Tesselation

A repeating pattern of figures that completely covers a plane, without gaps or overlaps.

You can create tesselations with translations, rotations and reflections.



A more understandable definition would be: Repeating a shape over and over to cover a plane without any sort of gap.

A great real life example of tessellations would be the graphics in games. Tessellation settings help the 3D image become more realistic by adding more polygons.
